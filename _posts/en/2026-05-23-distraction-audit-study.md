## Quick Summary

Recording the moment focus breaks reveals repeated triggers.

This routine is not decoration for a longer study session. It should leave **break moment** and **trigger** so the next session can decide what to repeat and what to reduce. Start with one subject and one unit before scaling it across a full schedule.

## Practical Routine

- Record when and why study broke.
- Remove the most frequent trigger first.
- Set up the environment before the next block.

## 40-Minute Session Example

If you only have 40 minutes today, start with 'Record when and why study broke'. Then record the **break moment** result and separate correct items from confused items. Use the final five minutes to write one question that starts the next review. That small closing record prevents the next session from becoming setup time again.

## Record Example

The record does not need to be long. Filling three fields, **break moment**, **trigger**, and **environment change**, is enough for one session. Move correct items to a longer interval, tag confused items with a short reason, and put missed items at the top of the next session. This keeps the next study block from starting with setup work.

## Checklist

- Before starting, define the **break moment** output for today.
- Before ending, check **trigger** and mark the next review item.
- Keep time spent, correct items, and missed items in one table.
- If the routine is too complex, remove one step and compare again next week.

## FAQ

### Should I apply Study Distraction Audit: Fix Environment and Triggers Before Willpower to every subject immediately?

Start with one subject, one unit, and one review cycle. Expand **Study Distraction Audit: Fix Environment and Triggers Before Willpower** only after the **break moment** record is useful in the next session.

### Can this work when study time is short?

Yes, if the short session still checks **trigger** and leaves a closing record. In **Study Distraction Audit: Fix Environment and Triggers Before Willpower**, time alone is not the point; retrieval, feedback, and rescheduling need to be included.

### Is Study Distraction Audit: Fix Environment and Triggers Before Willpower failing if scores do not improve immediately?

No. **Study Distraction Audit: Fix Environment and Triggers Before Willpower** first becomes valuable by revealing repeated failure points. Keep the same **break moment** measure for two or three weeks before changing the system.
