A study method becomes useful when it leaves an observable signal such as **pre-question**. This guide turns **Textbook Reading Routine: Leave Questions, Summary, and Examples** into a routine that can be tested in one session.

Textbook reading becomes study when it leaves pre-questions, post-reading summaries, and self-made examples.

## Quick Summary

Feeling familiar with a page is different from being able to explain it.

This routine is not decoration for a longer study session. It should leave **pre-question** and **section summary** so the next session can decide what to repeat and what to reduce. Start with one subject and one unit before scaling it across a full schedule.

## Practical Routine

- Turn headings into questions before reading.
- Close the book and summarize each section.
- Create one original example at the end.

## 40-Minute Session Example

If you only have 40 minutes today, start with 'Turn headings into questions before reading'. Then record the **pre-question** result and separate correct items from confused items. Use the final five minutes to write one question that starts the next review. That small closing record prevents the next session from becoming setup time again.

## Record Example

The record does not need to be long. Filling three fields, **pre-question**, **section summary**, and **self example**, is enough for one session. Move correct items to a longer interval, tag confused items with a short reason, and put missed items at the top of the next session. This keeps the next study block from starting with setup work.

## Checklist

- Before starting, define the **pre-question** output for today.
- Before ending, check **section summary** and mark the next review item.
- Keep time spent, correct items, and missed items in one table.
- If the routine is too complex, remove one step and compare again next week.

## FAQ

### Should I apply Textbook Reading Routine: Leave Questions, Summary, and Examples to every subject immediately?

Start with one subject, one unit, and one review cycle. Expand **Textbook Reading Routine: Leave Questions, Summary, and Examples** only after the **pre-question** record is useful in the next session.

### Can this work when study time is short?

Yes, if the short session still checks **section summary** and leaves a closing record. In **Textbook Reading Routine: Leave Questions, Summary, and Examples**, time alone is not the point; retrieval, feedback, and rescheduling need to be included.

### Is Textbook Reading Routine: Leave Questions, Summary, and Examples failing if scores do not improve immediately?

No. **Textbook Reading Routine: Leave Questions, Summary, and Examples** first becomes valuable by revealing repeated failure points. Keep the same **pre-question** measure for two or three weeks before changing the system.
